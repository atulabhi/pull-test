December 2017

OpenEBS Hackfest - A Big Hit
 
After receiving an overwhelming response from our contributors for Hacktoberfest 2017, OpenEBS recently launched it’s very first edition of OpenEBS Hackfest from Nov 15, 2017 to Dec 15, 2017.
We got awesome PRs from all around the world. The winner of the Hackfest is David Steiman from Berlin. Many thanks to everyone for all your contributions and for being awesome. Keep hacking and keep contributing!

  OpenEBS Hangout #4

Another OpenEBS Hangout was held on 22nd December 2017 where we demoed cMotion, which is the live movement of stateful workloads across any Kubernetes clusters, whether running on a cloud or onpremise.  In this case we showed PostgreSQL workloads moving from an AWS Kubernetes environment to a Google cloud environment without any loss of transactions.We also discussed some new capabilities of 0.5 and new directions for the OpenEBS project and for MayaOnline.io.  There were some great questions and comments from OpenEBS users. Click here to watch the recorded session and slides.

We were at Kubecon 2017, Texas

Kubecon was held from December 6th to 8th 2017 in Austin. In Kubecon, we launched MayaOnline, a software as a service solution that promises to address cloud lock-in while providing Kubernetes monitoring and control, focusing on the data layer.  We also talked quite a bit about our contributions to KubeCtl itself and spent a lot of time with OpenEBS users and friends in the Kubernetes community.
We also survived a Texas snow storm!

To know more visit the website https://mayaonline.io

Installing OpenEBS using StackPointCloud Trusted Charts

StackPointCloud (SPC) introduced a concept of Trusted Charts, a list of validated Helm Charts provided by its partners to quickly spin up a solution in a Kubernetes cluster. OpenEBS is integrated into SPC using these helm charts. If you are on SPC, it is now very easy to install OpenEBS on your K8s cluster and get started. Read more
